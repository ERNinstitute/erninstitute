Advancing the Science of Experience-Related Neuroplasticity

The ERN Institute studies how life experiences—such as language use, education, training, health events, and social environments—reshape brain function, cognition, and behavior across the lifespan.

Our work integrates behavioral science, EEG/MRI, and advanced quantitative methods to understand how experience builds resilience, cognitive reserve, and adaptive change.

About the ERN Institute

The ERN Institute (Experience-Related Neuroplasticity Institute) is a independent research and consulting initiative focused on how experience shapes the organization of the brain and cognitive systems over time.

The Institute supports research, collaboration, methodological leadership, and partnerships across academic, clinical, and industry domains.

Key Areas of Work

Experience & Brain

How complex experiences—language, education, training—shape brain networks, neural efficiency, and signal complexity.

Cognition & Aging

How lifelong experiences contribute to cognitive reserve, resilience, and dementia trajectories.

Methods & Measurement

Innovations in meta-analysis, measurement reliability, and continuous experience modeling.
